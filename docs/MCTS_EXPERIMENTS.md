# Policy-Value MCTS experiments

## Setup

- Checkpoint: `ppo_v3_semantic_population_iter0018.pt`
- Opponent: `RuleAgent`
- Policy actions: stochastic
- PUCT: `c_puct=1.25`, `max_depth=12`, `max_actions=16`
- Main experiments: 500 games in each seat (1,000 total)
- Intervals: unpaired Wilson 95%; the native battle engine does not expose a
  reproducible battle seed, so the CLI seed controls agent and determinization
  RNG only.

## Simulation-budget ablation

| Simulations | P0 win | P1 win | Overall | 95% CI | Wall time |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 8 | 72.0% | 46.0% | 59.0% | [55.9%, 62.0%] | 316 s |
| 16 | 76.6% | 51.2% | 63.9% | [60.9%, 66.8%] | 513 s |
| 32 | 74.6% | 53.2% | 63.9% | [60.9%, 66.8%] | 905 s |

The useful knee is 16 simulations. Going from 8 to 16 gained 4.9 percentage
points; 32 doubled most of the search cost without changing the pooled result.
Two earlier 500-per-seat stochastic policy panels scored 57.3% and 58.9%
against RuleAgent, so 16/32-simulation MCTS has a roughly five-point positive
point estimate over the direct policy. Those panels are independent rather
than paired, so this is not yet a causal effect estimate.

## Determinization ablation

| Determinizations | Budget/tree | P0 win | P1 win | Overall | 95% CI | Max depth | Wall time |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1, fixed oracle deck | 16 | 72.4% | 52.6% | 62.5% | [59.5%, 65.4%] | 12 | 724 s |
| 2, fixed oracle deck | 8 | 71.4% | 46.8% | 59.1% | [56.0%, 62.1%] | 8 | 751 s |
| 4, fixed oracle deck | 4 | 76.8% | 45.4% | 61.1% | [58.0%, 64.1%] | 4 | 868 s |
| 4, four-deck belief | 4 | 71.8% | 49.2% | 60.5% | [57.4%, 63.5%] | 4 | 872 s |
| 2, fixed oracle deck (32 total) | 16 | 75.6% | 49.8% | 62.7% | [59.7%, 65.6%] | 12 | 907 s |

At a fixed total budget of 16, independent determinizations fragment the tree
too aggressively. The confidence intervals overlap, but the single tree has
the best point estimate and lowest cost. Multi-determinization should therefore
keep a minimum per-tree budget instead of dividing an already small budget.
The 32-total, two-tree control restored depth 12 and reached 62.7%, essentially
the same point estimate as the 16-total single tree (62.5%) with longer wall
time. It found no measurable benefit from averaging two deep determinizations.

In the sample-mirror belief arm, 83.6% of 43,368 samples used the exact sample
deck and 16.4% used the closely related public MCTS sample deck. The incompatible
Meta A and Fishcat hypotheses were eliminated by visible evidence.

## Cross-deck belief screen

The model kept the sample deck while RuleAgent actually played Meta A. Each arm
used 100 games per seat, 16 total simulations and four determinizations.

| Opponent prior | P0 win | P1 win | Overall | 95% CI |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Wrong fixed sample prior | 68.0% | 67.0% | 67.5% | [60.7%, 73.6%] |
| Correct Meta A oracle | 65.0% | 67.0% | 66.0% | [59.2%, 72.2%] |
| Four-deck belief | 69.0% | 67.0% | 68.0% | [61.2%, 74.1%] |

The belief arm selected Meta A for all 11,540 determinization samples, so deck
identification works. The win-rate intervals overlap heavily and even the wrong
prior was not measurably worse. At this search depth, opponent-deck knowledge is
not the dominant performance bottleneck.

## Current decision

Use 16 simulations and one determinization as the default local MCTS setting.
Keep the belief implementation and candidate-deck interface, but do not claim a
win-rate improvement from belief yet. The next high-value work is deeper search
with batched/cached neural evaluation, a stronger/calibrated leaf value, or
Expert Iteration targets generated by the search.

One public notebook reports a sham-search shift in a shared-process arena. Code
inspection shows the supplied native engine allocates separate `BattleStart`
and search `AgentStart` game objects, while search descendants share the
search-side RNG stream. Before treating small deltas as final, reproduce the
placebo under a Kaggle-faithful one-process-per-agent evaluator.
